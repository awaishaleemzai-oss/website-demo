Why this exists

A directory, not a platform.

Sab Seekhein doesn't host any courses itself — it points to the free ones that already exist and are worth your time.

Pakistan has no shortage of free educational resources — government TeleSchool broadcasts, HEC's Coursera partnership, foundations like Sabaq and Taleemabad, and global platforms like Khan Academy and MIT OpenCourseWare. The problem has never really been supply. It's that these resources are scattered across dozens of websites, apps, and government portals, and a student trying to find the right one for their class often gives up before they find it.

Sab Seekhein (Urdu for "everyone learns") is an attempt to put them in one place, organised the way a student's actual journey unfolds — by class level, not by subject or brand name. Nothing on this site requires a subscription, a credit card, or even an account. If a resource later starts charging or shuts down, it should be removed; if you notice one that has, please tell us.

This is a static, independent site. It isn't affiliated with the Higher Education Commission, any provincial government, or any of the platforms listed here — we simply link out to their official pages.
